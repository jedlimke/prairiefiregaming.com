In Den of Wolves: Infinite Domain, you won't just be along for the ride—you'll be making the decisions that determine whether humanity survives or falls.

You might find yourself commanding the ICSS AEGIS, humanity's last battleship and the sole military force protecting the fleet. As Admiral, XO, or CAG, you'll coordinate fighter squadrons, deploy Marines, manage shield projectors, and make split-second tactical decisions while the wolves close in.

The AEGIS: Humanity's last line of defense

More Than Combat: This Is About Command

But military might isn't everything. The fleet operates under civilian government—the Council sets policy, the President leads, and you serve them. Most of the time.

When crisis strikes and there's no time to consult the Council, the AEGIS must act independently. And when it's over? You'll answer for those decisions.

This is the tension at the heart of Den of Wolves: You have the firepower, but not unlimited authority. You must balance duty, survival, and the fragile political order holding this desperate fleet together.

Every Role Matters

Maybe you're not military at all. You might be:

  • The President, navigating political factions while trying to hold civilization together
  • A Council member, debating who gets scarce resources and where the fleet jumps next
  • A ship captain, managing fuel, water, and desperate refugees while coordinating with the AEGIS
  • An engineer, keeping critical systems online as they break down under strain
  • A logistics officer, juggling impossible supply chains across a dozen vessels
  • A wolf, working to ensure everything falls apart from within

Every role is interconnected. Military decisions ripple through the civilian fleet. Political debates affect combat readiness. Resource shortages force impossible choices. Trust no one.

The fleet is counting on you.
