The responsibility the Aegis has found itself with is a great one. The Aegis is the sole battleship in the Survivor Fleet, and is responsible for its safety and security. This responsibility has fallen to you, and it is now your duty to ensure this little slice of humanity survives.

The Admiral's job is to make the high-level decisions and conduct important discussions and diplomacy amongst the fleet – especially liaising with the President. They are also responsible for the shield projector and weapon batteries should the XO need to direct the ship's complement of Marines.

The XO is the second-in command and has direct control of the Aegis' Marine squads. When the Admiral is off ship, which is fairly often, they are functionally in command of the Aegis.

The Commander - Air Group (CAG) is in direct control of the Fighter Squadron Units, and should manage their positioning and maintenance, as well as managing the pilots. They also control the Aegis' fighters during battles.

The Comms Officer is responsible for communicating with the rest of the fleet – primarily assisting the Admiral in learning of what is going on and informing other vessels of important information. They are also 3rd in command if the Admiral and XO are off the ship or otherwise indisposed.

The Logistics Officer is responsible for ensuring the Aegis itself has all the food, water, fuel and materials it needs. They may also assist other fleet vessels in obtaining these critical resources. They are also ultimately in control of the transport shuttles but this responsibility can be delegated.

The Council Liaison is authorized to speak and act on behalf of the Admiral in the Council. They may speak at Council sessions but do not have a vote. They should be updating the Aegis crew on events unfolding in the civilian government realm.

All of these roles can take on other duties if they have time (a good idea would be circulating around the room and trying to stay up to date on everything going on).

The Admiral and everyone serving on the Aegis are bound to serve the Council. Everyone on the Aegis' crew has made a pledge to serve the Council to the best of their abilities. However, in emergency scenarios, when it is clear that there is not enough time to liaise with the Council, and no relevant instruction has been given beforehand, the Aegis will be expected to make independent short-term decisions or take instruction from the president's office directly. However, you will be held accountable once the situation is over.

Declaring full martial law and superseding the civilian government is technically possible, but would be unthinkable in all but very extreme circumstances – unjustified attempts to do this would undoubtedly see split loyalties in the Aegis' pilot and marine personnel.

The Survivor Fleet has established law and order, and a balance of power similar to before 'The Attack'. However, considering the situation, almost anything could occur, and our little slice of humanity could be about to see any number of changes. You must ensure that the fleet survives and that the established law and order are not lost.

**Reminder** - The Admiral, XO, and CAG must report to the Fleet Map during Wolf attacks to direct the Aegis' weapon systems and fighters.

Objectives

• Support and serve the Council.

• The Fleet must survive.

• Work to ensure the Aegis is best able to provide safety and security for the fleet going forward. The safety of the fleet depends on not just maintaining the safety of the fleet, but actively seeking out ways that the Aegis can improve its procedures and capabilities (both political and physical).

Special rules -

Aegis Damage:

Most vessels take a -1 morale roll modifier per 1 damage taken to the vessel in a turn, for the Aegis, this is per 3 damage taken in a turn, rounded up. Any remainders still contribute to the modifier – e.g. 1 or 2 damage would be a -1 modifier, 4 or 5 damage would be a -2 modifier, etc

Shielding:

The Aegis has a unique action card that can be used at the start of a Wolf attack to shield one quadrant. The Admiral (or whoever is representing the Aegis at the Fleet Map) can then choose to have the Aegis take any damage suffered by other ships in that quadrant. Be careful with using this and ensure that the Aegis is well repaired before taking on additional damage.

Marines:

The ICS Marine Corps troops onboard the Aegis and Javelin are elite soldiers with superior training and weaponry. They get +2 to all combat against on-foot units. This bonus does not apply to dispersing riots however. The XO is responsible for command of the Marines, and during Wolf Attacks, they will be helping to coordinate transfer of these troops to other ships to help.
