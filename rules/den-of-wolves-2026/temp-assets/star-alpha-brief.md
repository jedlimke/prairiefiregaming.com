When the Star Alpha first joined the fleet, many suggested that a small luxury vessel designed for political figures was useless and that it should be scrapped for parts. However, as the political situation stabilised, it was argued that the President and their staff would need a place to live where they could remain on neutral ground, not living amongst the people (and security) of any one nation or the military. In addition, the vessel's high levels of armour meant that it could help to spread attacking Wolf forces thin whilst being relatively quite easy to repair.

The Presidential Team: You are the remnants of the civilian government of the united human race. The actual President died in the firestorm of The Attack, as did their cabinet. The highest ranking official from the government has just been sworn in as The President, and they quickly selected a Vice President and a small cabinet. The entirety of the judicial system aside from the Proximan Inquisitor is gone. Lawyers are in short supply (some would say this is a good thing). Traditional policing is now the task of the internal security forces onboard individual ships who are loyal to their captains, not to the public at large. The task of maintaining law and order is now your duty. Lead this little slice of humanity – not just to survive, but to a better future in the infinite domain of space.

You are the Executive branch of the government while the Council is the legislative and the Inquisitor has self-appointed themselves as judge and jury combined. So far the checks and balances from prior to The Attack have held the day, although there are elements in the fleet calling for additional Executive power to better respond to the seemingly daily crises.

The President's job is to make the decisions and conduct important discussions and diplomacy in the Council itself. The responsibility of the President is a great one. They are primarily responsible for the enforcement of laws that the Council passes. They are also the Commander in Chief of the military, so the Admiral answers to the President with regards to military action. Finally, they are the one who signs legislation passed by the Council into actual Law. 

The Vice President will be the President's eyes and ears "on the ground", talking to other teams. Remaining in close contact with the Admiral of the Aegis will likely be very important, as will liaising with The Parliamentarian. The Vice President is the tie-breaking vote in situations where the Council vote is tied. 

The Chief of Staff's job is to ensure the Star Alpha itself has enough food, water, fuel and materials to keep going, as well as manage security and any other operations – though they are also free to go around the room and talk politics if time allows. They should also be assisting the President and Vice President with law enforcement as needed.

The Parliamentarian is responsible for writing the verbiage of potential laws and placing this legislation in front of the council to vote on. They are themselves a member of the council and they vote on legislation. 

Review the Background Guide for information on the seven "nations" that now make up humanity. Your government does not consider any of them strict "allies", and the situation is far too dire for international rivalries to be tolerated. However, the President's political power base was largely drawn from the electorate in the FAS as they were originally a well regarded legislator from that great old power, and loyalty to your home nation is hard to let go of.

The Survivor Fleet has established law and order, and a balance of power similar to before 'The Attack'. However, considering the situation, almost anything could occur, and our little slice of humanity could be about to see any number of changes. You must ensure that the fleet survives and that the established law and order is not lost.

Objectives

• Work to ensure that the Presidency and the Council are the predominant force of power and influence in the fleet going forward. The safety of the entire fleet depends on the strength of this government.

• Keep good relations with the Aegis and ensure they are working for the Council. The security of the fleet is paramount – but not at the expense of giving up civilian governmental authority.

• The fleet must survive, together.

The Council

Note that only the 7 council members (7 representatives, one each from the FAS, CPA, SAN, Rosal, Gliese, Proxima, and the Parliamentarian) along with the junior member from Helvetii, the President, Vice President, and Aegis liaison may officially speak in the council. No one other than the News Media may observe council proceedings. 

Issues

There are a number of existing pressing issues in the Fleet that have many people concerned. Control will explain these to the President, who can choose to add them to the agenda for Council meetings.
