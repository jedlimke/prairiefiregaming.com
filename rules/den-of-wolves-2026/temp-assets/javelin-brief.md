The Javelin is one of the newest generation of missile boats commissioned and built for the ICS. It was on a shakedown trip when The Attack happened, and its crew is green but eager for payback against The Wolves. Its guided anti-ship missiles are powerful but costly to produce. It also has cannon batteries and a shuttle. As a smaller vessel, The Javelin is reliant on fleet tender resupply for its crew's needs; there are no onboard food or water synthesizers.

The command structure before The Attack saw the Javelin's captain taking orders from the Admiral. That person is dead, and the current Admiral was previously a lower ranked commander, same as your captain. Your loyalty ultimately lies with the civilian government, but the acting Admiral may give you orders as well.

Having said that, politics has no place in war. Your crew were first-hand witnesses to the Wolves' savagery. You watched from the orbit of Jupiter as Old Earth burned. As an attack vessel, your mission is to hunt and kill the enemy. Protecting the fleet is important, but if there is an opportunity to strike at the heart of the Wolves' war machine, do it.

The Captain is responsible for directing the day to day operations of the ship. They are responsible for taking over the weapon batteries and missile launchers should the XO need to coordinate a Marine response against Wolf boarders.

The XO is the second-in command and has direct control of the Javelin's Marine squads. When the Captain is off ship, they are functionally in command of the Javelin.

The Staff Sergeant is a battle hardened soldier who keeps their soldiers alive. The Marines might take orders from the XO, but it's their Sarge they listen to. They have some special abilities that are helpful in a fight.

**Reminder** - The Captain and XO must report to the Fleet Map during Wolf attacks to direct the Javelin's weapon systems.
Objectives

• Protect the fleet. Use your missiles wisely, they are expensive to produce.

• Find any Wolf sympathizers and prosecute them with extreme prejudice.

• You are not as strong as the Aegis, but your ship is well armored and punches above its weight. Ensure your voice is heard with the Admiral when it comes to tactical and strategic matters.

Special Rules

Anti-Ship Missiles:

The Javelin was chosen as the testbed for these new prototypes. They are very accurate but their warheads can be finicky and damage is unpredictable. As long as there is a crew on the Missile Launcher station, each round during combat, the Javelin's Captain may choose to spend a missile to deal D6 damage to any target in any quadrant.

Marines:

The ICS Marine Corps troops onboard the Aegis and Javelin are elite soldiers with superior training and weaponry. They get +2 to all combat against on-foot units. This bonus does not apply to dispersing riots however. The XO is responsible for command of the Marines, and during Wolf Attacks, they will be helping to coordinate transfer of these troops to other ships to help.
